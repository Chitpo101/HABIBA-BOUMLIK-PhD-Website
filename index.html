About Me

Photo Placeholder
View My CV

I received my Ph.D. in Social and Cultural Anthropology from the University of Strasbourg, France in 1996. I also hold an M.A. in Arabic and Islamic Studies from the University of Strasbourg and a B.A. in French as a Foreign Language from the University of Besançon, France.

My academic background and teaching experience include Arabic, French Language and Francophone Cultures and Literatures, Linguistics, Cultural Anthropology, Women Cross-Culturally, Culture and Society in the West, Middle Eastern History, and MENA (Middle East and North Africa) Cinema.

Prior to my current position at LaGuardia, I taught in France, Hungary, Egypt, and in several colleges and universities in New York.

Pedagogy

Teaching Philosophy

I conceive my teaching philosophy as a journey through which my students and I embark for one semester. I guide them to overcome obstacles and I cherish the end of their journey where, in language courses for example, they can give presentations, converse in the target language and measure their learning progress.

My holistic teaching approach does not separate a language from its social and historical contexts.

My goal has always been to bring my students to better understand and to question some implicit cultural assumptions. I strive to promote cross-cultural awareness and appreciation in the diverse courses I have been teaching.

The Language Career Fair

The Language Career Fair further explores options for students and brings awareness about the importance of languages in the job market. Guest participants hail from publishing, health, business, tourism & hospitality, interpreting, law, education, and finance, from large and small corporations and from institutions such as the UN.

Language Career Fair
The International Mother Language Day

The International Mother Language Day has been celebrated by the United Nations since 2000. The goal of the IMLD is to celebrate the rich diversity of LaGuardia. As languages are the most powerful instruments of preserving and developing our heritage, by promoting mother tongues, we develop awareness of linguistic and cultural traditions in the college community.

International Mother Language Day
Marseille Study Abroad Program (June–July 2017)

I co-led this program with my friend and colleague Lucy McNair (English department). Combining a one-week intensive course at LaGuardia and a two-week stay in Marseille, participants studied the city as a "contact zone" through the lens of French literature in translation and creative nonfiction as they explored the city's institutions and neighborhoods.

Marseille Study Abroad
African Heritage Celebration with Mookie Wilson (May 2014)

I invited the New York Mets World Series Hero Mookie Wilson to speak about "Sports and Race in America" and I moderated a Q&A with Mr. Wilson on the lack of involvement of African Americans in baseball compared to the past, as well as on the prevalence of stereotypes and prejudice against Blacks in baseball.

African Heritage Celebration
Heritage Language Scholars, Henry Luce Foundation (2014–2015)

The HLS was a year-long program designed to enhance language proficiency and global competencies of selected students. The program aimed to develop leadership skills through weekly seminars; develop participants' heritage language and cultural knowledge; and link HL skills, academic and career opportunities through summer internships overseas or in the U.S.

New York Forum of Amazigh Film

NYFAF
New York Forum
of Amazigh Film

The annual New York Forum of Amazigh Film (NYFAF) is a showcase of contemporary feature, documentary, and short films about the Amazigh people of North Africa and in the diaspora.

NYFAF's mission is to create a space where the filmmakers, artists, and scholars of indigenous Amazigh identity and culture can gather yearly to share their knowledge and enthusiasm while fostering dialogue with a diverse audience of students and thinkers gathered from across New York City and the world.

Through pre- and post-screening Q&As, live performances, and exhibitions of art and artifacts, the New York Forum of Amazigh Film seeks to disseminate Amazigh cinema and promote an understanding of the unique history, culture, and language of Amazigh peoples in North Africa and in the diaspora.
